# User Research

## Research Goal

Understand how PMs and UX researchers can use AI to review interviews faster while preserving evidence, uncertainty, and human judgment.

## Personas

### Persona 1: Product Manager

Needs to convert qualitative feedback into prioritized product opportunities. Cares about pain points, evidence, user impact, and next actions.

### Persona 2: UX Researcher

Needs to synthesize interviews without losing nuance. Cares about quotes, context, participant segments, caveats, and auditability.

### Persona 3: AI Product Team

Needs to demonstrate how AI can assist research workflows safely. Cares about structured output, reviewability, and avoiding overclaiming.

## Jobs To Be Done

- When reviewing a long interview, help me quickly find high-friction moments.
- When reading an AI-generated insight, show me the exact evidence behind it.
- When preparing a product review, turn raw quotes into actionable opportunities.
- When uncertainty is high, help me ask better follow-up questions instead of overclaiming.

## Pain Points

- Interview review is time-consuming.
- Emotion and hesitation signals are easy to miss.
- AI summaries are often too generic.
- Product teams need evidence, not only conclusions.
- Research findings need to translate into decisions.

## Interview Analysis Template

Each insight should contain:

- Insight title
- Summary
- Supporting evidence
- Pattern
- User impact
- Product impact
- Confidence level
- Design opportunity
- Risks / caveats
- Recommended follow-up

## Research Assumptions

- Evidence-grounded insights will be trusted more than black-box summaries.
- PMs want product opportunities, not raw emotion labels.
- Open-vocabulary emotion labels are more useful than rigid sentiment categories.
- Human review should remain part of the workflow.
