# Competitive Analysis

## Positioning

AffectInsight sits between sentiment analysis tools, user research repositories, conversation analytics, and AI product workflow prototypes.

It is not trying to be the most accurate emotion classifier. It is designed to show how AI-generated user insights can be evidence-grounded, auditable, and product-actionable.

## Comparison Table

| Product / Category | Strength | Limitation | AffectInsight Differentiation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Generic AI summary tools | Fast summaries | Often lack evidence grounding | Every insight links to quotes and cues |
| Sentiment analysis APIs | Simple text scoring | Rigid positive/negative labels | Open-vocabulary emotion signals |
| User research repositories | Strong storage and tagging | Less AI-native synthesis | Demo focuses on AI-assisted synthesis and opportunity extraction |
| Conversation analytics | Useful dashboards | Often operational, not PM-focused | PM-ready pain points and product opportunities |
| Academic affect recognition | Strong model framing | Hard to productize safely | Responsible AI boundaries and product docs |

## Product Wedge

The wedge is not "emotion recognition." The wedge is "evidence-backed insight generation for user research workflows."

## Risks

- Users may over-trust emotion labels.
- Mock metadata can be mistaken for real model outputs.
- Affect inference can be culturally biased.
- Sensitive use cases require stronger governance.

## Strategic Takeaway

The most defensible product framing is:

> I designed a product-safe, evidence-grounded interview insight workflow for PMs and UX researchers, including app demo, PRD, metrics, responsible AI constraints, and basic tests.
